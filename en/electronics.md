# Electronics Basics

Understanding basic electronics and circuitry helps you grasp how computer hardware works. This chapter offers suggestions for getting started with electronics.

## Analog Circuits

- **Voltage and current**: Ohm’s law, resistors, capacitors and other fundamentals;
- **Basic components**: diodes, transistors and operational amplifiers—their principles and applications.

## Digital Circuits

- **Boolean algebra**: AND, OR, NOT, XOR and other logical operations;
- **Combinational logic**: adders, decoders, encoders, multiplexers and more;
- **Sequential logic**: flip‑flops, registers, counters and finite state machines.

## Introductory Resources

- **Textbooks**: *Microelectronic Circuits* by Sedra & Smith; *Digital Design and Computer Architecture* by Harris & Harris;
- **Online courses**: MIT’s 6.002 *Circuits and Electronics*; digital logic courses on edX;
- **Projects**: Build simple circuits using simulation software (e.g., LTSpice) or development boards (e.g., Arduino).

Understanding these fundamentals will make it easier to grasp system‑level courses such as computer architecture and embedded systems.
