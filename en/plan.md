# CS Learning Plan

Below is an example learning pathway for computer science. Adjust it according to your background and goals:

1. **Mathematics Basics**: First solidify your understanding of calculus, linear algebra and probability, which form the basis for algorithms and machine learning. (See [Mathematics Basics](math-basic.md))
2. **Programming Basics**: Choose a programming language (Python or C/C++ is recommended) and learn basic syntax, data structures and algorithmic thinking. (See [Programming Basics](programming-basic.md))
3. **Data Structures & Algorithms**: Study common data structures and classic algorithms systematically, and learn how to analyze time and space complexity. (See [Data Structures & Algorithms](data-algorithm.md))
4. **Computer Systems**: Understand computer organization, operating systems, networks and databases to build a foundation for higher‑level topics. (See [Computer Systems](computer-systems.md))
5. **Software Engineering**: Master the software development process, version control, testing and design patterns to improve engineering skills. (See [Software Engineering](software-engineering.md))
6. **Advanced Topics**: Depending on your interests, delve into artificial intelligence, machine learning, deep learning, compilers and more.
7. **Continuous Learning**: Keep up with the latest technologies and continuously update your knowledge. Also focus on personal growth and study methods. (See [Personal Growth](growth.md))

This route serves as a general reference; adjust the sequence and pace as needed.
