# Recommended Books

Quality textbooks and reference books are essential for systematic learning. This section recommends classic titles across mathematics, computer foundations, algorithms and engineering practice.

## Mathematics

- **Calculus** (e.g., *Calculus* by Stewart): comprehensive coverage of limits, derivatives, integrals and more.
- **Linear Algebra and Its Applications** by Lay: explains vectors, matrices, linear transformations and eigenvalues.
- **Introduction to Probability and Statistics** (e.g., by Mendenhall): introduces random variables, distributions, expectations, etc.

## Computer Science Foundations

- **The Art of Computer Programming** by Donald Knuth: a monumental series on algorithm analysis, suitable for deep study.
- **Introduction to Algorithms** by Cormen et al.: covers a wide range of algorithms and includes exercises to systematically improve your algorithmic skills.
- **Computer Systems: A Programmer’s Perspective** by Randal E. Bryant: shows how programs run on real machines, from hardware to operating systems.
- **Operating System Concepts** by Silberschatz et al.: covers principles and design of modern operating systems.
- **Computer Networking: A Top‑Down Approach** by Kurose & Ross: explains networking from the application layer downward.

## Programming and Software Engineering

- **The C Programming Language** by Kernighan & Ritchie: the classic guide to C that helps you understand low‑level concepts.
- **Python Crash Course** by Eric Matthes: a practical introduction to Python programming.
- **Code Complete** by Steve McConnell: discusses best practices for software development and code quality.
- **Design Patterns: Elements of Reusable Object‑Oriented Software** by Gamma et al.: introduces 23 classic design patterns.

These recommendations are for reference only; choose books according to your interests and learning objectives.
