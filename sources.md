# Information sources

High‑quality information sources can help you stay up to date with academic developments and the latest research. This page collects some commonly used information channels.

## Online platforms

- **Academic search engines**: such as arXiv, Google Scholar, used to search for papers.
- **Q&A communities**: Stack Overflow, Zhihu, etc., convenient for discussion and problem‑solving.
- **Online forums**: relevant sections on V2EX, Reddit, etc.

## News and blogs

- Tech news sites that track new trends in artificial intelligence and machine learning.
- Excellent personal or team blogs sharing algorithms and practical experience.

## Conferences and journals

- Follow top conferences (such as NeurIPS, ICML, ACL, etc.) and journals in your field to obtain the latest research results.
- Tips on how to find conference papers and watch conference videos.

## Communities and organizations

- Participate in open‑source communities and study groups to improve efficiency through co‑learning.
- Follow academic accounts on social media to get timely information.
